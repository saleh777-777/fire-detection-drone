EMBER

Autonomous thermal vision for early wildfire detection.

A drone built to spot a fire's heat signature before smoke ever reaches the sky — scanning day or night, faster than a foot patrol or a satellite pass.

THE PROBLEM

Fires don't wait to be noticed.

A wildfire can grow from a smoldering ember into an uncontrollable blaze within minutes. Detection still relies heavily on watchtowers, ranger patrols, and satellites that may only pass over a given area a few times a day — and can be blocked by smoke or limited after dark.

By the time a fire is reported, the window for a fast, low-cost response has often already closed.

THE SOLUTION

A drone that watches for heat, not smoke.

EMBER pairs a thermal sensor with a YOLO-based detection model on an autonomous drone, so it can find a fire's heat signature directly — before visible smoke even appears.

  1. 01Sense — the thermal camera continuously scans the terrain below for unusual heat.
  2. 02Detect — an onboard YOLO model classifies hotspots in real time and flags likely fires.
  3. 03Respond — coordinates are logged immediately so ground teams can move fast.
